## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **[10] Baroni, Romano, Toni, Aurisicchio, Bertanza 2015** — Origin of the QBAF formalism and DF-QuAD's lineage. Mandatory reading; AAEs are defined directly on this substrate.
- **[38] Rago, Toni, Aurisicchio, Baroni 2016** — Introduces DF-QuAD ("Discontinuity-Free QuAD") explicitly. Required to understand why AAE's `lim_{ε→0}` is well-defined.
- **[16] Chi et al. 2021** — Source of the 48-argument fraud detection QBAF used as case study 3 (SM appendix C). Useful as a benchmark.
- **[17] Cocarascu, Rago, Toni 2019** — Source of the movie recommender QBAF (case study 2). Defines Argumentative Dialogical Agents.
- **[28] Kotonya & Toni 2019** — Source of the fake news QBAF (case study 1).
- **[29] Lundberg & Lee 2017** — SHAP, source of the *missingness* property and the comparison target for "exponential vs linear" runtime claim.
- **[42] Shrikumar et al. 2017** — Integrated Gradients / DeepLIFT precursor, source of the *completeness* / efficiency property.
- **[18] Covert, Lundberg, Lee 2021** — Removal-based explanation framework, source of the *counterfactuality* property.
- **[35] Potyka, Yin, Toni 2022** — Same authors' prior work on quantitative faithfulness; the property catalogue here is partially adapted from this.
- **[3] Amgoud & Ben-Naim 2018** and **[32] Potyka 2018** — Future-work targets: Euler-based and quadratic-energy gradual semantics for AAE generalisation.
- **[31, 33] Mossakowski & Neuhaus / Potyka** — Non-convergence of iterative DF-QuAD on cyclic QBAFs; directly motivates the acyclic restriction.
- **[22] Delobelle & Villata 2019** — Closely related interpretability work for gradual semantics; project already has notes on this paper.
