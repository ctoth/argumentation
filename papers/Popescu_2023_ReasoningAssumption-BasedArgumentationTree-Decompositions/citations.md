## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Lehtonen, Wallner, and Jarvisalo 2021 [48]**: Directly relevant prior work on declarative algorithms and complexity for ABA; this paper compares against that state-of-the-art ASP-based line.
- **Fichte et al. 2021 [34]**: Decomposition-guided reductions for argumentation and treewidth; important alternative to D-FLAT-style DP for preserving bounded width through solver reductions.
- **Lampis, Mengel, and Mitsou 2018 [47]**: QBF route that the authors identify as a possible way to get tighter runtime bounds than generic MSO/Courcelle arguments.
- **Dewoprabowo et al. 2022 [23]**: Practical dynamic-programming counting for Dung extensions, useful for understanding how the ABA approach relates to abstract-argumentation DP.
- **Cerutti et al. 2018 [15]**: Implementation foundations for formal argumentation; background for placing this ABA solver work in the broader implementation landscape.
