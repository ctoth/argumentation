Develops tree-decomposition based reasoning algorithms for flat finite assumption-based argumentation frameworks, with MSO/Courcelle FPT results for credulous and skeptical acceptance under admissible, complete, stable, and preferred semantics.
The paper gives a D-FLAT dynamic-programming construction using witness and counterwitness table entries, plus experimental evidence that the approach is promising for counting-heavy ABA tasks.
It is directly relevant to project work on implementing structured argumentation reasoning with explicit rule derivability, contraries, attacks, and bounded-width execution strategies.
