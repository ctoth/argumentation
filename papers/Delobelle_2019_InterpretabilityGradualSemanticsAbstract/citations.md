## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **[2] Amgoud, Ben-Naim, Vesic (2017)** — *Measuring the intensity of attacks in argumentation graphs with Shapley value*. The closest prior contribution-measure work; Delobelle/Villata explicitly position their per-argument impact as more general (covers all arguments, not just direct attacks; works for semantics beyond syntax-independent + monotonic). Direct comparison anchor for the QBAF/contribution-function workstream (Wave 1 #2/#3).
- **[1] Amgoud, Ben-Naim (2016)** — *Axiomatic foundations of acceptability semantics*. Source of the Killing/Weakening axioms whose insufficiency motivates the Balanced Impact property.
- **[8] Cayrol, Lagasquie-Schiex (2013)** — *Bipolarity in argumentation graphs*. Cited as the support-relation source needed to extend the framework to bipolar AFs (future work). Already on Wave 1 list (#4).
- **[10] Dung (1995)** — Foundational AF paper. Background.
- **[16] Pu, Luo, Zhang, Luo (2015)** — *Counting semantics*. The CS used as the central BI-satisfying example. Required to implement to apply Defs. 7-11.
- **[13] Miller (2019)** — *Explanation in AI from social sciences*. Source of the interpretability definition used in the introduction.
