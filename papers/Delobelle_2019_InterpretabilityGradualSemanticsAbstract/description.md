Defines a deletion-based impact measure for gradual semantics in abstract argumentation and a Balanced Impact (BI) property that lets the acceptability degree of an argument decompose additively into per-argument impacts.
Shows the Counting Semantics satisfies BI while the h-categorizer does not, gives an ACY tree-unfolding algorithm extending the additive decomposition to cyclic AFs, and uses impact rankings to identify the most positive and most negative impacting arguments for interpretability and debate strategy.
Direct foundation for any per-argument explanation story over weighted/gradual semantics in the argumentation library, and the anchor paper for the QBAF contribution-function follow-ups (Al Anaissy et al. 2024).
