## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Dung (1995)** — Foundational AF paper; every definition of conflict-free / admissible / preferred / grounded traces back here. Anyone reading this paper without that one will mis-state the semantics.
- **Caminada (2006)** and **Dunne & Caminada (2008)** — Semi-stable semantics; provides Σ₂ᴾ-completeness used in Table 2 and motivates the saturation encoding.
- **Bench-Capon (2003)** — VAFs; Section 4.1 of this paper depends on its definitions and the attack-vs-defeat distinction for stable semantics.
- **Cayrol & Lagasquie-Schiex (2005)** — BAFs and the safe / closed-under-support refinements; Section 4.2 follows their definitions exactly.
- **Eiter & Polleres (2006)** — Saturation technique in ASP; the conceptual basis for the preferred/semi-stable encodings.
- **Lifschitz & Turner (1994)** — Splitting Theorem; underlies almost every correctness proof in Section 3.
- **Faber & Woltran (2009)** — Ideal-semantics encoding implemented in ASPARTIX but not described in this paper.
- **Nieves et al. (2008, 2009)** and **Wakaki & Nitta (2008)** — Closest competing ASP-based encoding work; comparing those approaches against ASPARTIX is essential context.
