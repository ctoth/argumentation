The ASPIC+ counterpart of the ASPforABA work by the same group: a direct declarative approach that encodes reasoning in an instantiation of the rule-based ASPIC+ framework (without preferences) into answer set programming, avoiding materialization of the potentially exponentially many arguments. It rephrases argumentation semantics over assumptions -- pairs (P, D) of ordinary premises and defeasible rules -- proves a correspondence between sigma-assumptions and sigma-extensions of the corresponding abstract AF for admissible, complete, preferred and stable semantics, and gives composable ASP modules (a shared common module plus small per-semantics add-ons, with ASPRIN for preferred). It encodes the three ASPIC+ attack forms (undercut, rebut, undermine), derives NP/coNP/Sigma-2-p complexity results for the instantiation, and scales empirically to frameworks with thousands of atoms. Relevant to this project as the reference for the same-group ASP-encoding methodology and as the path to structured argumentation beyond ABA toward ASPIC+.
