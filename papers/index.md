# Paper Index

## [Skeptical Reasoning with Preferred Semantics in Abstract Argumentation without Computing Preferred Extensions](Thimm_2021_SkepticalReasoningPreferredSemantics/notes.md)  (abstract-argumentation, sat-solving, preferred-semantics, ideal-semantics)
Presents CDAS, a SAT-call algorithm for skeptical preferred acceptance that avoids computing preferred extensions. Also derives CDIS, a related ideal-extension algorithm based on preferred-super-core pruning. This is the main implementation source for replacing native `DS-PR` and `SE-ID` enumeration paths in the solver.

## [Fudge: A light-weight solver for abstract argumentation based on SAT reductions](Thimm_2021_FudgeLight-weightSolverAbstract/notes.md)  (abstract-argumentation, sat-solving, solver-architecture)
Describes Fudge, a lightweight C++ SAT-based abstract-argumentation solver. Its central engineering choices are standard SAT reductions for easy tasks, novel encodings for preferred skeptical and ideal reasoning, and incremental CaDiCaL calls. It supports our plan to refactor the package SAT helpers into an incremental task-directed backend.

## [ArgSemSAT-1.0: Exploiting SAT Solvers in Abstract Argumentation](Cerutti_2015_ArgSemSAT-1.0ExploitingSATSolvers/notes.md)  (abstract-argumentation, sat-solving, preferred-semantics)
Describes ArgSemSAT, a C++/Glucose solver built around SAT encodings of complete labellings. It gives a compact implementation model for complete, grounded, preferred, stable, and acceptance tasks. It supports the package direction of making complete labellings the shared SAT surface.

## [argmat-sat: Applying SAT Solvers for Argumentation Problems based on Boolean Matrix Algebra](Pu_2017_ArgmatSatApplyingSATSolver/notes.md)  (abstract-argumentation, sat-solving, semi-stable-semantics, stage-semantics)
Describes argmat-sat, a Boolean-matrix/SAT solver for ICCMA tasks. Its key project value is the range-vector encoding and assumption-space procedure for semi-stable and stage semantics. It supports the next workstream step of replacing range-maximal native acceptance with task-directed SAT loops.

## [Computing Preferred Extensions in Abstract Argumentation: a SAT-based Approach](Cerutti_2013_ComputingPreferredExtensionsAbstract/notes.md)  (abstract-argumentation, sat-solving, preferred-semantics, solver-architecture)
Presents PrefSat, a SAT-based algorithm for enumerating preferred extensions of finite abstract argumentation frameworks by searching over complete labellings. The paper classifies complete-labelling SAT encodings, proves the enumeration algorithm correct, and reports that a Glucose-backed implementation substantially outperforms ASPARTIX/dlv, ASPARTIX-META, and NOF on 2,816 random AFs. It is directly useful for designing and testing SAT-backed preferred-extension enumeration in this project's argumentation solver work.

## [Complexity-Sensitive Decision Procedures for Abstract Argumentation](Dvorak_2014_ComplexitySensitiveDecisionProcedures/notes.md)  (abstract-argumentation, sat-solving, complexity-sensitive, preferred-semantics)
Presents complexity-sensitive decision procedures for hard abstract-argumentation reasoning tasks under preferred, semi-stable, and stage semantics. It contributes fragment and distance-based complexity results, then instantiates them as a SAT-based CEGAR-style framework implemented in CEGARTIX. It is relevant to this project because it gives concrete base-semantics encodings, oracle-call structure, and benchmark evidence for incremental SAT-backed argumentation reasoning.

## [ConArg: a Tool to Solve (Weighted) Abstract Argumentation Frameworks with (Soft) Constraints](Bistarelli_2012_ConArgToolSolveWeighted/notes.md)  (weighted-argumentation, abstract-argumentation, constraint-programming, argumentation-semantics, solver-architecture)
This paper presents ConArg, a Java/JaCoP constraint-programming tool for computing classical and weighted abstract argumentation extensions. It contributes explicit CSP and semiring-based SCSP encodings for Dung-style semantics, weighted alpha-extensions, beta-grounded problems, graph generation, and benchmarks against ASPARTIX. It is relevant as an implementation-oriented bridge between argumentation semantics, soft constraints, and practical solver architecture.

## [Answer-set Programming Encodings for Argumentation Frameworks](Egly_2010_Answer-setProgrammingEncodingsArgumentation/notes.md)  (argumentation, asp, dung, semantics, implementation)
The canonical ASPARTIX paper. Presents fixed-query disjunctive-datalog (ASP) encodings for the principal Dung AF semantics — conflict-free, stable, admissible, complete, grounded, preferred, semi-stable — plus value-based and bipolar generalisations, packaged over DLV. Each module's data complexity matches the natural class of the encoded reasoning problem (P/NP/coNP/Σ₂ᴾ/Π₂ᴾ); Σ₂ᴾ-hard semantics use disjunction + saturation rather than per-instance recompilation. Direct reference for the ASP-backend workstream and a template for encoding new semantics.

## [Approximating Operators and Semantics for Abstract Dialectical Frameworks](Strass_2013_ApproximatingOperatorsSemanticsAbstract/notes.md)  (adf, semantics, approximation-fixpoint-theory, logic-programming, dung-af)
Embeds Brewka & Woltran's abstract dialectical frameworks (ADFs) into Denecker-Marek-Truszczyński approximation-fixpoint theory via a single characteristic operator G_Ξ whose fixpoints uniformly yield all major ADF semantics, including new admissible/preferred/semi-stable/stage/naive variants for non-bipolar ADFs. Proves polynomial faithful translations ADFs↔LPs and AFs↔LPs (standard and Dung encodings shown equivalent), locates AFs⊂ADFs⊂LPs, and shows the operator collapse SF_Θ=F_Θ explaining why AFs cannot distinguish supported from stable. Canonical reference for ADF semantics infrastructure and ASP encodings of preferred/semi-stable/stable AF reasoning.

## [Characterizing Strong Equivalence for Argumentation Frameworks](Oikarinen_2010_CharacterizingStrongEquivalenceArgumentation/notes.md)  (abstract-argumentation, strong-equivalence, kernels, semantics)
Characterizes strong equivalence for Dung AFs across all major semantics (stable, preferred, complete, admissible, grounded, semi-stable, ideal, eager). Identifies kernel operators — syntactic transformations removing redundant attacks from self-attacking arguments — such that two AFs are strongly equivalent iff their kernels are identical: a-kernel for stable/preferred/semi-stable/ideal/eager, a*-kernel for admissible/complete, separate characterization for grounded. Foundational for any AF rewriting, simplification, or substitution-safe optimization in the solver.

## [AGM Meets Abstract Argumentation: Contraction for Dung Frameworks](Baumann_2019_AGMContractionDung/notes.md)  (abstract-argumentation, agm, belief-revision, contraction, dung-logics)
Completes the AGM trilogy (expansion, revision, contraction) for Dung AFs using "Dung logics" — monotonic logics whose models are AFs and whose consequence relation captures strong equivalence. Reformulates classical AGM contraction postulates for the AF setting; proves the negative result that Harper Identity fails (so recovery-satisfying contraction operators do not exist in general), then shows that dropping the (independently controversial) recovery postulate restores well-behaved contraction. Direct implications for any system implementing AF change operations.

## [On the Bipolarity in Argumentation Frameworks](Amgoud_2004_BipolarityArgumentationFrameworks/notes.md)  (bipolar-argumentation, abstract-argumentation, gradual-semantics, decision-making, foundations)
Origin point of the bipolar AF line. Introduces bipolarity into Dung-style abstract argumentation by adding an explicit support relation R_sup alongside defeat R_def, giving `<A, R_def, R_sup>`. Identifies three layers where bipolarity matters (framework structure, gradual valuation, selection), defines branch-based direct/indirect defeaters/defenders/supporters, proposes local (P1–P3) and global (Pg1–Pg4) axioms for compensation-style gradual valuation, and sketches a desire/sub-desire/partial-plan/complete-plan vocabulary. Foundational for any project work that encodes BAFs or compares gradual semantics across bipolar variants.

## [Interpretability of Gradual Semantics in Abstract Argumentation](Delobelle_2019_InterpretabilityGradualSemanticsAbstract/notes.md)  (gradual-semantics, interpretability, abstract-argumentation, counting-semantics, h-categorizer)
Defines a deletion-based impact measure for gradual semantics in abstract argumentation and a Balanced Impact (BI) property letting an argument's acceptability degree decompose additively into per-argument impacts. Shows Counting Semantics satisfies BI while h-categorizer does not, gives an ACY tree-unfolding algorithm extending the additive decomposition to cyclic AFs, and uses impact rankings to identify most positive/negative impacting arguments for interpretability. Direct foundation for any per-argument explanation story over weighted/gradual semantics.

## [Argument Attribution Explanations in Quantitative Bipolar Argumentation Frameworks](Yin_2023_ArgumentAttributionExplanationsQuantitative/notes.md)  (qbaf, gradual-semantics, explanation, attribution, df-quad)
Defines Argument Attribution Explanations (AAEs) as gradient-based attribution scores `∇|_{B↦A} = ∂σ(A)/∂τ(B)` over arguments in acyclic QBAFs under DF-QuAD, with closed-form direct (Prop 2) and indirect chain-rule (Prop 4) expressions computable in O(n) time. Proves six properties (explainability, missingness, completeness, counterfactuality, agreement, monotonicity) for single-path connections and gives counterexamples showing failures under multifold connectivity, validated with case studies in fake-news, movie recommendation, and a 48-argument fraud-detection QBAF. Anchor paper for any quantitative explanation workstream over the project's QBAF substrate.

## [Contribution Functions for Quantitative Bipolar Argumentation Graphs: A Principle-based Analysis](Kampik_2024_ContributionFunctionsQuantitativeBipolar/notes.md)  (quantitative-argumentation, bipolar, explainability, contribution-functions, principle-based-analysis)
Defines four contribution functions (Removal, Removal-without-indirection, Shapley value, Gradient) quantifying how one argument influences another's final strength in acyclic QBAGs, proves which functions satisfy which of five principles (Contribution Existence, Quantitative Contribution Existence, Directionality, (Quantitative) Local Faithfulness, (Quantitative) Counterfactuality) under five gradual semantics. Headline: no function satisfies all principles — Counterfactuality and Local Faithfulness are inherently in tension — but each "main" function uniquely characterises one principle (Removal↔Counterfactuality, Shapley↔Quantitative Contribution Existence, Gradient↔Local Faithfulness). Methodological template for principle-based analysis of any reduction or encoding.

## [Declarative Algorithms and Complexity Results for Assumption-Based Argumentation](Lehtonen_2021_DeclarativeAlgorithmsComplexityResults/notes.md)  (assumption-based-argumentation, asp, complexity, structured-argumentation)
Presents direct ASP encodings for ABA and ABA+ reasoning in the flat logic-programming fragment, avoiding translation through abstract argumentation frameworks. Establishes complexity results showing that ABA+ reverse attacks induced by preferences raise several verification and acceptance tasks, while stable-semantics acceptance remains at the ABA complexity level. Relevant to this project's backend because it gives solver-oriented representations, polynomial subchecks, oracle-level complexity expectations, and benchmark evidence for ABA/ABA+ implementation choices.

## [Reasoning in Assumption-Based Argumentation Using Tree-Decompositions](Popescu_2023_ReasoningAssumption-BasedArgumentationTree-Decompositions/notes.md)  (aba, treewidth, dynamic-programming, structured-argumentation)
Develops tree-decomposition based reasoning algorithms for flat finite assumption-based argumentation frameworks, with MSO/Courcelle FPT results for credulous and skeptical acceptance under admissible, complete, stable, and preferred semantics. The paper gives a D-FLAT dynamic-programming construction using witness and counterwitness table entries, plus experimental evidence that the approach is promising for counting-heavy ABA tasks. It is directly relevant to project work on implementing structured argumentation reasoning with explicit rule derivability, contraries, attacks, and bounded-width execution strategies.

## [On the Computational Complexity of Assumption-Based Argumentation for Default Reasoning](Dimopoulos_2002_ComputationalComplexityAssumption-basedArgumentation/notes.md)  (aba, computational-complexity, nonmonotonic-reasoning, preferred-semantics, default-reasoning)
Classifies the complexity of credulous and sceptical reasoning for ABA instances of theorist, circumscription, logic programming, default logic, and autoepistemic logic under admissibility and preferability semantics. Shows that the new semantics often match or exceed stable-extension complexity, reaching `Pi^p_4`-completeness for AEL sceptical preferred reasoning. Relevant to this project because it gives solver-capability boundaries for ABA/default-reasoning semantics and warns that admissible/preferred reasoning is not automatically a cheaper implementation target.

## [A generalised framework for dispute derivations in assumption-based argumentation](Toni_2013_GeneralisedFrameworkDisputeDerivations/notes.md)  (assumption-based-argumentation, proof-procedures, dispute-derivations, structured-argumentation)
A unified framework for ABA dispute derivations that parameterizes GB-, AB-, and IB-dispute procedures as instances of X-dispute derivations. It proves soundness and completeness for grounded, admissible, ideal, and related semantics, and extends the framework to structured X-dispute derivations that explicitly compute arguments and attacks. It is directly relevant to implementing a single reusable ABA proof engine that can emit both assumption supports and provenance-bearing dialectical structures.

## [SCC-recursiveness: a general schema for argumentation semantics](Baroni_2005_SCC-recursivenessGeneralSchemaArgumentation/notes.md)  (abstract-argumentation, scc-recursiveness, argumentation-semantics, solver-architecture, cf2-semantics)
Introduces SCC-recursiveness, a general schema that computes any Dung admissibility-based semantics by decomposing the defeat graph into strongly connected components, processing them in topological order, and applying a semantics-specific base function to each single-SCC sub-framework while injecting parent SCCs' choices as a defeated/defended/provisionally-defeated partition. It proves stable, complete, preferred, and grounded are all SCC-recursive (each with an explicit base function), shows weak base-function constraints guarantee conflict-freeness and grounded agreement, and defines four new semantics (AD1, AD2, CF1, CF2) that repair preferred semantics' asymmetric cycle handling. It is the theoretical foundation for this project's core/scc_recursive.py and the direct precedent for wiring query-directed SCC decomposition into SAT-based acceptance solving.

## [mu-toksia: An Efficient Abstract Argumentation Reasoner](Niskanen_2020_MuToksiaEfficientAbstractArgumentationReasoner/notes.md)  (abstract-argumentation, sat-solving, solver-architecture, incremental-sat, dynamic-argumentation, cegar)
Describes mu-toksia, the C++ SAT-based abstract-argumentation reasoner that won all main-track reasoning tasks at ICCMA 2019. Its central architectural choice is instantiating a single SAT solver once per execution and driving every reasoning task (DC, DS, SE, EE) through incremental solve-under-assumptions calls against one persistent clause database. It gives explicit SAT encodings of conflict-free, admissible, complete and stable sets, and reduces the harder tasks to reused primitives: grounded via unit propagation, DS-CO via grounded, stable primed by the grounded lower bound, and DS-PR / semi-stable / stage via the Dvořák et al. CEGAR loop. Dynamic AFs are covered by conditioning each toggleable attack on a selector variable used as a SAT assumption. It is the reference "single incremental SAT solver" precedent for this project's task-directed af_sat backend.

## [Declarative Algorithms and Complexity Results for Assumption-Based Argumentation](Lehtonen_2021_DeclarativeAlgorithmsComplexityABA/notes.md)  (assumption-based-argumentation, aba, aba-plus, asp, answer-set-programming, solver-architecture, complexity, iccma)
The JAIR 2021 foundation of ASPforABA (winner of the ICCMA 2023 ABA track), extending the authors' AAAI 2019 paper. It develops a direct declarative approach to reasoning in the logic-programming fragment of ABA and its preference extension ABA+, encoding reasoning tasks straight into answer set programming instead of materializing arguments or translating to abstract AFs. Semantics are restated over assumption sets via forward-derivability and encoded as small composable ASP modules: a shared common module guesses an in/out set and computes support/defeat/conflict-freeness, and stable/admissible/complete/preferred/grounded/ideal are thin add-ons; credulous and skeptical acceptance are single constraints; preferred uses ASPRIN subset-maximization; grounded is an explicit fixpoint iteration; ideal wraps CLINGO cautious reasoning in a refinement loop. It also settles new ABA+ complexity results and gives corrected grounded/ideal algorithms. Primary reference for this project's ABA solver.

## [An Answer Set Programming Approach to Argumentative Reasoning in the ASPIC+ Framework](Lehtonen_2020_ASPApproachArgumentativeReasoningASPICplus/notes.md)  (structured-argumentation, aspic-plus, asp, answer-set-programming, solver-architecture, defeasible-reasoning)
The ASPIC+ counterpart of the ASPforABA work by the same group: a direct declarative approach that encodes reasoning in an instantiation of the rule-based ASPIC+ framework (without preferences) into answer set programming, avoiding materialization of the potentially exponentially many arguments. It rephrases semantics over assumptions (pairs of ordinary premises and defeasible rules), proves a correspondence between assumption sets and AF extensions for admissible/complete/preferred/stable, gives composable ASP modules (shared common plus per-semantics add-ons, ASPRIN for preferred), encodes the three ASPIC+ attack forms (undercut, rebut, undermine), and scales empirically to thousands of atoms. Relevant as the reference for the same-group ASP-encoding methodology and the path to structured argumentation beyond ABA.

## [Crustabri (ICCMA 2023 solver description)](Mailly_2023_CrustabriRustArgumentationReasoner/notes.md)  (abstract-argumentation, sat-solving, solver-architecture, incremental-sat, rust, iccma, stub)

## [Reasoning in Assumption-Based Argumentation via SAT](Niskanen_2025_ReasoningAssumptionBasedArgumentationSAT/notes.md)  (assumption-based-argumentation, sat, structured-argumentation, acyclicity, benchmarks)
This paper presents the first purely SAT-based reasoning approaches for flat logic-programming assumption-based argumentation (ABA), encoding the derivation-acyclicity constraint four ways: a level-based clause encoding, a compact vertex-elimination encoding, a graph-based acyclicity user propagator, and an unfounded-set source-pointer propagator, all on CaDiCaL via IPASIR-UP. Empirically the vertex-elimination (SAT-VE) and unfounded-set (SAT-UFS) variants match or beat the state-of-the-art ASP system ASPforABA on the ICCMA23 ABA benchmarks and a new CLUSTERED generator, including ~50% more solved instances on the beyond-NP ideal-extension task. It is the direct reference design for this project's ABA SAT encodings (aba_sat.py eager arc-acyclic stable, aba_acyc_sat.py cone/acyclicity prototype) and its source repo names the "100ba" branch shorthand.
