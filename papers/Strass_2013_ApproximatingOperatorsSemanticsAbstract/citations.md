## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **[3] Brewka & Woltran 2010 (KR)** — original ADF paper. Mandatory predecessor; defines ADFs, BW-stable, BW-admissible, BW-preferred, BW-grounded operator Γ_Ξ. Strass's whole paper restructures and generalises this work.
- **[11] Denecker, Marek, Truszczyński 2000** — foundational AFT paper introducing approximating operators, stable operator SO, well-founded semantics on bilattices. Direct algebraic engine of Strass's reconstruction. Also relevant: [12] AIJ 143 (uniform default+AEL treatment) and [13] Inf. Comput. 192 (ultimate approximation).
- **[14] Dung 1995 (AIJ 77)** — original AF paper. Defines AFs, characteristic function F_Θ, grounded/preferred/stable/complete extensions, and the two LP→AF translations Strass examines in §4.3.
- **[15] Egly, Gaggl, Woltran 2010** — ASP encodings for AFs. Together with Strass's Theorem 4.13/4.16 (PFM AF↔LP) this is the textbook foundation for our ASP-backend workstream (papers/Strass_2013... + papers/Egly_2010_AnswerSetProgrammingEncodings, task #9 in our queue).
- **[43] Wu, Caminada, Gabbay 2009** — first showed AF complete = 3-valued stable models in LPs. Strass extends to preferred (M-supported), semi-stable (L-supported), stable (2-valued supported) under both translations.
- **[5] Brewka, Ellmauthaler, Strass, Wallner, Woltran 2013** — "ADFs revisited" (IJCAI). Listed in §6 future work as the source of *ultimate* ADF semantics that Strass plans to compare with his approach.
