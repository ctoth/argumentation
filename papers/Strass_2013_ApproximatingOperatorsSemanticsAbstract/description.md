Strass embeds Brewka & Woltran's abstract dialectical frameworks (ADFs) into the Denecker-Marek-Truszczyński approximation-fixpoint-theory framework by defining a characteristic operator G_Ξ whose fixpoints uniformly yield all major ADF semantics, including new admissible/preferred/semi-stable/stage/naive variants for non-bipolar ADFs.
The paper proves polynomial, faithful, modular translations ADFs↔LPs and AFs↔LPs (both standard and Dung encodings, shown equivalent), locates AFs⊂ADFs⊂LPs in the nonmonotonic-reasoning landscape, and shows the operator collapse SF_Θ=F_Θ that explains why AFs cannot distinguish supported from stable.
This is the canonical reference for ADF semantics infrastructure in our argumentation library and underpins ASP encodings of preferred/semi-stable/stable AF reasoning (workstream-asp-backend).
