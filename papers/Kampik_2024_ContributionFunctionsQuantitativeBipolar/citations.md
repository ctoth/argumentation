## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **[13] Čyras, Kampik, Weng 2022** — "Dispute trees as explanations in quantitative (bipolar) argumentation," CEUR Workshop. The original workshop paper this paper extends and corrects (the four contribution functions and the original Contribution Existence principle were introduced here). Read first to understand the technical fix to Shapley and the principle re-statement.
- **[14] Yin, Potyka, Toni 2023** — "Argument attribution explanations in quantitative bipolar argumentation frameworks," ECAI 2023. Studies gradient-based contribution under DFQuAD in more depth; complementary deep dive.
- **[19] Delobelle, Villata 2019** — "Interpretability of Gradual Semantics in Abstract Argumentation," ECSQARU. Introduced the first two contribution functions in the abstract (non-bipolar) gradual setting; foundational lineage. (This paper is on the project's wave-1 list.)
- **[21] Amgoud, Ben-Naim 2018** — IJAR. Defines directionality and stability principles for weighted bipolar graphs that this paper inherits as Principles 2.1, 2.2.
- **[22] Potyka 2019** — Modular semantics framework for bipolar weighted argumentation, AAMAS. Provides the (aggregation, influence) decomposition Table 2 builds on.
- **[23] Baroni, Rago, Toni 2019** — IJAR. The "principled spectrum" template the contribution-function principle methodology echoes.
- **[27] Mossakowski, Neuhaus 2018** — Modular semantics framing used here; defines convergence behaviour important for cyclic-extension future work.
- **[32] Kampik, Čyras, Ruiz Alarcón 2023** — IJAR. Sister paper on *change*-based explanations (sufficient/necessary/counterfactual) for QBA; the "set explanation" complement to single-argument contributions.
- **[34] Amgoud, Ben-Naim, Vesic 2017** — IJCAI. Original Shapley-value treatment in an argumentation graph context; predecessor of Ctrb^S.
- **[38] Himeur, Yun, Bisquert, Croitoru 2021** — Agent-impact assessment in weighted bipolar AFs; closest aggregated-contribution analogue to counterfactual function.
