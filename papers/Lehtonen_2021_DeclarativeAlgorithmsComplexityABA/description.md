The JAIR 2021 foundation of ASPforABA (winner of the ICCMA 2023 ABA track), extending the authors' AAAI 2019 paper. It develops a direct declarative approach to reasoning in the logic-programming fragment of assumption-based argumentation (ABA) and its preference extension ABA+, encoding reasoning tasks straight into answer set programming instead of materializing arguments or translating to abstract AFs. Semantics are restated as conditions on assumption sets via forward-derivability and encoded as small composable ASP modules: a shared common module guesses an in/out assumption set and computes support, defeat and conflict-freeness, and stable/admissible/complete/preferred/grounded/ideal are thin add-ons; credulous and skeptical acceptance are single constraints; preferred uses ASPRIN subset-maximization; grounded is an explicit fixpoint iteration; ideal wraps CLINGO cautious reasoning in a refinement loop. The paper also establishes new ABA+ complexity results (admissibility verification coNP-complete, credulous admissible acceptance Sigma-2-p-complete) and gives preference-aware ASP encodings for ABA+ stable and grounded. It is the primary reference for this project's ABA solver: direct assumption-level encodings, the CEGAR/iterative-ASP structure, and the ideal/grounded algorithms.
