This 2004 NMR workshop paper introduces *bipolarity* into Dung-style abstract argumentation by adding an explicit support relation R_sup alongside the defeat relation R_def, giving a bipolar argumentation framework `<A, R_def, R_sup>` and identifying three layers where bipolarity matters: framework structure, gradual valuation of arguments, and selection of acceptable sets / complete plans. It defines branch-based direct/indirect defeaters, defenders and supporters, proposes local (P1–P3) and global (Pg1–Pg4) axioms for a compensation-style gradual valuation, and sketches a desire/sub-desire/partial-plan/complete-plan vocabulary culminating in a conflict-free maximal acceptability notion (Def.19). It is the origin point of the bipolar AF line later formalized by Cayrol & Lagasquie-Schiex 2005 and Amgoud et al. 2008, foundational for any project work that encodes BAFs into reductions or that compares gradual semantics across bipolar variants.
