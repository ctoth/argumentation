## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Dung 1995 — "On the acceptability of arguments…"** *Artificial Intelligence* 77. The base framework being extended; mandatory background.
- **Cayrol & Lagasquie-Schiex 2004 (IRIT report 2004-07-R) — "Bipolarité en argumentation".** Sister technical report by two of the same authors; the formal worked-out version of the present NMR sketch and the path to the 2005 ECSQARU paper already in the project collection.
- **Karacapilidis & Papadias 2001 — HERMES.** Cited as an early *implicit* bipolar argumentation system; useful as a worked applied bipolar setting if/when the project wants empirical bipolar examples.
- **Verheij 2002 (NMR'2002) — extension multiplicity in dialectical argumentation.** Adjacent NMR paper on multi-extension behaviour; useful for comparing how bipolar selection differs from purely defeat-based extension semantics.
- **Benferhat, Dubois, Kaci & Prade 2002 (KR'02) — bipolar representation in possibilistic logic.** External evidence that bipolar formalisms are useful elsewhere in KR; helps justify the cross-domain bipolarity argument.
